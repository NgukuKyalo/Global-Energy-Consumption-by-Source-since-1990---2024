This projects acts as a global energy consumption overview. Using Our World in Data (https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/global-energy-substitution) "Global Primary Energy Consumption" Dataset, with the goal of identifying trends on energy consumption over the years.

## Conclusion and Recommendation
This analysis explored global primary energy consumption from 1800 to 2024, revealing a world in the middle of a significant but incomplete energy transition.
Overall global energy consumption has grown dramatically since 1800, driven first by coal during the industrial revolution, then by oil and natural gas through the 20th century. The total energy demand has never stopped growing.
Since 1990, renewable energy has seen remarkable growth. Hydropower remains the most utilized renewable source in absolute terms due to its long history, however Solar and Wind have emerged as the fastest growing sources, with Solar growing over 3,000x since 1990 — the fastest growth rate of any energy source in history.
When indexed to 1990 as a baseline, the growth trajectory of Solar and Wind is extraordinary compared to Hydropower, which has grown modestly. This suggests that while Hydropower built the foundation for clean energy, Solar and Wind are driving the future.
Looking at the 2024 energy mix, fossil fuels still dominate global consumption, making up the largest share despite decades of renewable growth. This highlights that while the transition is happening, it still has a long way to go.
Comparing fossil fuels and renewables directly since 1990, fossil fuel consumption has continued rising in absolute terms, but renewables are closing the gap at an accelerating pace — particularly in the last decade.
The data tells a story of hope but also urgency — the clean energy revolution is real, but the world still runs overwhelmingly on fossil fuels.
